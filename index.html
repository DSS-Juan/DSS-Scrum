Section 2 — Friction points

Problem 1 — One week is not enough

Tasks get created on Wednesday and reviewed the following Wednesday. That leaves barely 4–5 working days to pick up a task, deal with blockers, iterate, and deliver something meaningful.

Everything feels urgent because nothing has room to breathe. The team is always playing catch-up instead of working ahead.

Section 3 — Scrum

What is Scrum?

Scrum is a simple framework that helps small teams move fast, stay aligned and continuously improve — without drowning in process.

It's built around short focused bursts of work called sprints, with built-in checkpoints so the team always knows what's happening and can course-correct quickly.

It doesn't replace the tools you use — it gives those tools a rhythm and structure that makes them actually work together.

Section 3 — Scrum

The three key ceremonies

1

Sprint planning — every other Wednesday

Decide as a team what we're committing to for the next 2 weeks. Pull from the backlog, assign tasks, set clear goals. AI notes → Trello cards ready to go.

~60 min
2

Daily standup — async or live

3 questions each day: What did I do yesterday? What am I doing today? Any blockers? Surfaces problems in hours, not days. Can be a quick Slack thread.

15 min/day
3

Sprint review + retrospective — Wednesday wk 2

What did we ship? (review, 30 min) — then — What went well, what didn't, what do we try next sprint? (retro, 30 min). The loop that makes the process smarter over time.

~60 min total

Why Scrum actually helps

🎯

Clarity on what matters

Each sprint has a defined goal. No ambiguity about what the team is working on or why.

🚨

Retrospectives mean every sprint is slightly better than the last. Compounding gains over time.

Section 4 — The proposal

What changes — and what stays the same

Before — current state

❌1-week sprint — not enough time to execute
❌One Wednesday meeting doing everything at once
❌No check-ins between meetings — blockers hide for days
❌No retrospective — same friction repeats every week
✅AI transcripts, Trello, automation already in place

After — proposed Scrum rhythm

✅2-week sprint — real time to plan, execute and deliver
✅Wed wk 1: planning only. Wed wk 2: review + retro
✅Daily async standup — 15 min, catch blockers early
✅Built-in retrospective every 2 weeks — process improves
✅Same AI transcripts, Trello and automation — unchanged
Section 4 — The proposal

How we start

1

This Wednesday — sprint planning session (60 min)

Use the AI meeting notes as our backlog. Define what we're committing to for the next 2 weeks. Assign Trello cards.

2

Daily — async standup (15 min)

Each team member posts 3 lines: done, doing, blocked. Slack thread or voice note — whatever works best.

3

Wednesday in 2 weeks — review + retro (60 min)

30 min: what did we ship? 30 min: what do we improve next sprint? Then straight into sprint 2 planning.

4

After 2 sprints — decide together

Run two full sprints then evaluate in a proper retrospective. No long-term commitment — just an experiment.
