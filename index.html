Section 2 — Friction points

Problem 1 — One week is not enough

Tasks get created on Wednesday and reviewed the following Wednesday. That leaves barely 4–5 working days to pick up a task, deal with blockers, iterate, and deliver something meaningful.

Everything feels urgent because nothing has room to breathe. The team is always playing catch-up instead of working ahead.

Section 3 — Scrum

What is Scrum?

Scrum is a simple framework that helps small teams move fast, stay aligned and continuously improve — without drowning in process.

It's built around short focused bursts of work called sprints, with built-in checkpoints so the team always knows what's happening and can course-correct quickly.

It doesn't replace the tools you use — it gives them a rhythm and structure that makes them work together.

🏃 Fast iterations 👀 Full visibility 🔧 Self-improving 🎯 Clear focus

Section 3 — Scrum

The three key ceremonies

1

Sprint planning — every other Wednesday

Decide what the team is committing to for the next 2 weeks. Pull from the backlog, assign tasks, set clear goals. AI notes → Trello cards ready to go.

~60 min
2

Daily standup — async or live

3 questions each day: What did I do yesterday? What am I doing today? Any blockers? Surfaces problems in hours, not days. Can be a quick Slack thread.

15 min/day
3

Sprint review + retrospective — Wednesday wk 2

What did we ship? (30 min review) — then — What went well, what didn't, what do we change? (30 min retro). The loop that makes the process smarter over time.

~60 min total

Section 4 — The proposal

How we start

1

This Wednesday — sprint planning (60 min)

Use AI meeting notes as our backlog. Define what we commit to for 2 weeks. Assign Trello cards.

2

Daily — async standup (15 min)

Each person posts 3 lines: done / doing / blocked. Slack thread or voice note — whatever works best.

3

Wednesday wk 2 — review + retro (60 min)

30 min: what did we ship? 30 min: what do we improve? Then straight into sprint 2 planning.

4

After 2 sprints — decide together

Run two full sprints then evaluate in a proper retro. No long commitment — just a 4-week experiment.
